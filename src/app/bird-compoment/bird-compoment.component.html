Description

The Canada Goose, Branta canadensis maxima, bred from central Manitoba to Kentucky but was nearly driven extinct in the early 1900s. Programs to reestablish the subspecies to its original range were in many places so successful that the geese have become a nuisance in many urban and suburban areas.

Habitat

While they are breeding, these geese live along the tundra in flood plains, ponds, or lakes. Most do not breed on the coast, but they do live near the coasts.

Food

Wood ducks eat nuts, seeds, vegetables, berries, insects, and they are predominantly omnivores.

Key Facts

The lesser snow goose stands 64 to 79 cm (25 to 31 in) tall and weighs 2.05 to 2.7 kg (4.5 to 6.0 lb). The larger subspecies, the greater snow goose (C. c. atlanticus), nests in northeastern Canada. It averages about 3.2 kg (7.1 lb) and 79 cm (31 in), but can weigh up to 4.5 kg (9.9 lb).
